Clip

Users of popular grid-based DAWs like Ableton or Bitwig may be familiar with clips. Clips are, simply put, a small piece of something playable that contains MIDI notes. A Song is what happens when you arrange multiple clips by Scene and Track in a grid.
  Patterns, transforms, and scales referenced clips are only symbolic links - which may make sense if you have a programming background. What this means is that if one has a pattern named "groove1" and later reconfigures what notes and chords are in groove1, Warp will change in all clips that use that pattern. This makes it easy to try out both small and large scale variations in a song without tedious reprogramming.

Basic Features

  • A clip belongs to a Scene (horizontally) and a Track (Vertically) in the Song grid.
  • A clip contains a list of one or more Patterns that play in order, resulting in notes.
  • Playing a clip will stop any other clips that are playing in the same Track as the clip.
  • A clip may have one or more Scales. These scales are used for each playing Pattern and are rotated in a loop as each pattern advances or the clip repeats.
  • If a clip does not have a Scale assigned, the Scale can come from the Scene, and if not set there, the Song.
  • A clip may also define a tempo multiplier rate such as 0.5 (half speed) or 2 (double speed)
  • A clip may have one or more Transforms, which are powerful tools that modify the currently playing Pattern. These are used in rotation just as the Scales are.
  • A clip may have a list of tempo_shifts, which are added or subtracted to the tempo of each playing Pattern. These rotate similarly as scales or transforms.
  • A clip may be set to repeat indefinitely (it will end if the scene ends), or for a finite number of times.
  • A clip may be set to advance the Scene when the clip completes. This is off by default.
  • A clip may trigger another specific clip when it completes. This does not occur if the clip is set to advance a Scene.
